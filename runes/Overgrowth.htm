Notes[edit | edit source]

  • Overgrowth only counts unit deaths the champion has direct line of sight on. The effect's radius is reduced when there is terrain or nearsight blocking direct vision on dying units. Shared vision with other allied units observing the dying units is ignored.
    • PENDING FOR TEST:: Interaction with shared vision of an allied champion that has Overgrowth as well.
  • The percentage bonus after 15 stacks is split into a base and bonus health portion, just like the pre-V8.23 version of Overgrowth and an old version of Gargoyle Stoneplate at the same time.
    • This was explicitly done to prevent bonus health scalings via abilities such as Feast from strongly benefitting health scalings.
    • Because of this, base health scalings such as Guardian Angel's Rebirth health and Friend of the Forest's health cost are increased by the modifier.
    • Tooltips do not properly recognize the modified base stats, so when they generate and calculate bonus health as the champion's total health minus their base health at current level, they incorrectly show higher values from bonus health scalings than will actually be used by the effect.(bug)
  • Overgrowth users may still gain the effect while dead.

Nexus Blitz differences edit
  • Bonus maximum health changed to 80 monsters or minions.
  • Flat health per stack changed to 5.

Trivia[edit | edit source]

  • This rune has a similar effect to  Strength of the Ages, a Season 2016 Summoner Mastery keystone. Compared to that keystone, Overgrowth stacks significantly slower but its bonus does not have a cap.

Sight
Sight is a property of units, structures, certain summoner spells, items, and abilities that represents a team possessing vision of a target area or unit. It is always center range. A line of vision cannot extend beyond terrain or an unrevealed brush.

Sight can also refer to an effect that makes a unit visible regardless of the fog of war or brush, allowing them to be targeted. Standard sight cannot see stealthed units, but True Sight can.

  • Nearsight is a crowd control debuff that inhibits sight; allied vision is lost in addition to a reduced sight radius.
  • Obscured vision is similar to sight in a way that it can observe units, but does not render units targetable, rather it exposes their position and informs of their presence.
  • Unobstructed vision is a form of sight that allows a unit to be able to see their full vision radius, ignoring any obstructions that would otherwise restrict visibility.
  • All effects that use unit-targeting require sight of the target. Delayed unit-targeted attacks and spells can cancel if sight of the selected target is suddenly lost.

League of Legends content
Nearsight
A unit that is nearsighted has their sight radius reduced and cannot see beyond their sight radius.

While nearsighted, units cannot reveal stealthed wards or traps. Nearsighted units lose vision of all their allies as well as all external sources of vision (including from wards or abilities). This prevents unit-targeted effects, including basic attacks, from being used outside of the unit's reduced sight radius.
True sight and unobstructed vision do not bypass the vision blocked by nearsight.

Sources of nearsight do not stack with each other; only the application with the greatest sight reduction will take effect.

Interrupts: Interrupts target commands if the nearsighted unit's target is outside of their new vision area and prevents automated target acquisition.
Reduction: The duration is unaffected by Tenacity.
Resist: Resisted by crowd control immunity with the exceptions of Fiora's Riposte and Morgana's Black Shield.
Removal: Removed by Cleanses with the exceptions of Cleanse and Mikael's Blessing.

  • Crowd control applied by one's self or an ally cannot be countered in any way, neither reduced in duration, resisted, nor removed unless special cased to do so.
    • The crowd control effect may still be cleansed if it is applied as a debuff separate from the originated ability.
